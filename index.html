Subject's Stats

Name: Cassandra Oswald || Alias: Thursday || Model Age: Five || Model Gender: Female


F.A.I. Model 37X Appearance

Model 37X has been adapted to fit the proportions of young Miss Oswald when she was still in the prime of her health and youth. Its height is five-feet-five-inches, and we have managed to achieve a semi-realistic weight with machinery and metal alloy, causing the vessel to weigh in at roughly one-hundred-twenty pounds. A work of art and a piece of equipment in equal parts, despite being comprised of machinery almost entirely the F.A.I. was specifically designed to appear to have human anatomy, if only for aesthetic. An energy core hides within her chest, encased by a metal box forged in the shape of a human heart. This theme extends for every vital piece of her functioning mechanisms, tucked within organ shaped chromatic cases. Her face is blank, molded after Miss Oswald’s in shape, but intentionally severely lacking in defining details. She is more akin to a blank doll/puppet, while the screen built into her synthetic flesh is supposed to fill in the blanks, allowing for incredible amounts of customization if she wishes to make changes to herself.

Subject's Behavior

Positive signs of adaptation and free will. Subject is extremely perceptive and learns at a rate that is to be expected by a computerized Artificial Intelligence. She can break down problems fast, has an exceptional memory bank, and retains the skill to solve issues as they arise by using what she’s learned. Additionally her ability to make decisions for herself is uncanny, truly a remarkable specimen, closer to human than any specimen we’ve managed to produce so far. Drawbacks are an extremely logical mind; subject is constantly under stress, worrying three steps ahead of herself. She always takes the most reasonable route, in place of making decisions based on passion or emotion, suggesting more bugs in our programming. Further testing required.
